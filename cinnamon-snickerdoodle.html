Cinnamon Snickerdoodles Recipe with Ceylon Cinnamon

You know those cookies that taste like a warm hug? That’s a snickerdoodle. Soft centers, lightly crisp edges, and a sparkly cinnamon-sugar coat that makes them impossible to stop at one. This version uses the delicate aroma of Ceylon cinnamon for a clean, fragrant finish that pairs perfectly with tea, coffee—or a late-night snack straight off the tray. Let’s bake!

Snickerdoodle Ingredients

  • 1 cup (230 g) unsalted butter, softened (not melted)
  • 1½ cups (300 g) white sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2¾ cups (345 g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp cream of tartar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp fine salt
  • ¼ cup (50 g) white sugar
  • 1½ Tbsp ground Ceylon cinnamon

Step-by-Step Instructions for Cinnamon Snickerdoodles

  1. Preheat & pan: Heat oven to 190°C / 375°F. Line a baking tray with parchment.
  2. Cream: Beat butter and sugar until pale and fluffy. Add eggs and vanilla; mix until smooth.
  3. Dry mix: In a separate bowl, whisk flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt. Fold into the butter mixture just until a soft dough forms—don’t overmix.
  4. Roll & coat: Stir sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl. Scoop 1 Tbsp dough, roll into balls, and coat generously in cinnamon sugar.
  5. Bake: Arrange 5 cm (2 in) apart. Bake 8–10 minutes until edges set but centers still look soft.
  6. Cool: Rest on the tray 5 minutes, then move to a rack. Try not to eat them all immediately.

Pro Tips for Perfect Snickerdoodle Cookies

  • For extra chew, bake until just set; they finish as they cool.
  • Prefer a stronger aroma? Add an extra ½ Tbsp of Ceylon cinnamon to the coating.
  • Chill the dough 20 minutes if it feels too soft or warm.
