What is Ceylon Pepper?

Ceylon pepper (Piper nigrum) is cultivated in the lush hills of Sri Lanka. Known for its sharp heat and citrusy undertones, it is prized as one of the world’s finest peppers.

  • High in piperine, delivering bold flavor
  • Complex aroma with earthy, citrus notes
  • Hand-harvested and sun-dried for maximum potency

Benefits

Bold Flavor Enhancer

Brings heat and depth to almost every cuisine.

Rich in Antioxidants

Piperine offers natural health-supporting properties.

Global Culinary Staple

From fine dining to home kitchens, pepper is essential.

Culinary Uses

Seasoning

Freshly ground over dishes for maximum aroma.

Cooking

Used in curries, marinades, soups, and sauces.

Gourmet Blends

Mixed with other spices for complex profiles.

How to Identify Authentic Pepper

Uniform Size

High-quality Sri Lankan peppercorns are well-rounded.

Strong Aroma

Fresh, sharp scent when crushed.

Dark Black Color

Indicates premium drying methods.

Storage Tips

  • Keep whole peppercorns airtight and away from sunlight.
  • Grind fresh for best flavor; use within 18–24 months.
  • Pre-ground pepper should be used within 6–8 months.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Sri Lankan pepper stronger than others?

Yes, it is known for its sharpness and citrusy depth.

Black or white pepper?

Black is bold; white is milder and suited for light sauces.

Can pepper lose flavor?

Yes, if stored ground for too long. Always grind fresh for best results.
