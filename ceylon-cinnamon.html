What is Ceylon Cinnamon?

Ceylon cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum), often called True Cinnamon, is prized for its light color, soft texture, and naturally sweet, delicate flavor. It’s hand-crafted into thin, multi-layered quills and traditionally harvested in Sri Lanka.

  • Gentle, elegant flavor profile (low heat, sweet aroma)
  • Fine, thin quills that crumble easily
  • Popular for daily tea, desserts, and refined savory dishes

Benefits

Delicate, Sweet Aroma

Enhances desserts, tea, and coffee without overpowering other flavors.

Grades Explained

Ceylon cinnamon is graded by quill thickness and appearance. Thinner quills generally indicate a more delicate flavor profile.

Alba

Finest & Thinnest

Ultra-thin quills with a diameter of just 6–7 mm, offering a subtle sweetness—ideal for premium tea and desserts.

C5

Premium Grade

Fine-textured cinnamon with an 8–9 mm diameter, delivering gentle sweetness—popular for export and daily use.

C4

Balanced

Slightly thicker quills measuring 10–12 mm, warm and aromatic—great for cooking and baking.

M5

Everyday

Medium-grade cinnamon with a 16–18 mm diameter, offering a rich, comforting flavor—ideal for larger batches.

Ceylon vs Cassia

Feature Ceylon (True Cinnamon) Cassia
Flavor Light, sweet, nuanced Strong, spicy, more pungent
Texture Thin, many layers, crumbles easily Thick, hard bark, fewer layers
Color Light tan to honey brown Reddish dark brown
Typical Use Tea, desserts, refined dishes Bold savory dishes, strong spice blends

Tip: If the quill is thin with multiple layers like a cigar—it's likely Ceylon.

How to Identify Authentic Ceylon Cinnamon

Multi-Layer Quills

Cross-section shows many thin layers, not a single thick piece.

Soft & Crumbly

Breaks easily by hand—unlike hard, woody cassia sticks.

Light Honey Color

Lighter shade with a fine, paper-like bark.

Delicate Aroma

Sweet, floral notes over sharp spice.

Storage & Freshness

  • Store in an airtight container away from heat, light, and moisture.
  • Whole quills keep aroma longer than powder; grind small amounts as needed.
  • For best flavor, use within 12–18 months (quills) or 6–12 months (powder).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ceylon cinnamon good for daily use?

It’s favored for its gentle flavor and is commonly used daily in tea and food. For specific health concerns, ask a qualified professional.

What’s the difference between Alba and C5?

Alba is the thinnest, most delicate quill; C5 is slightly thicker and very popular for everyday premium use.

Can I cook savory dishes with Ceylon cinnamon?

Absolutely. It complements curries, stews, rice, and spice blends without overpowering them.

Whole quills or powder?

Quills keep longer and can be simmered and removed; powder delivers instant flavor but should be used sooner for peak aroma.
