What is Ceylon Cardamom?

Ceylon cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum) is grown in the cool highlands of Sri Lanka. Known as the “Queen of Spices,” it carries a sweet, floral, and slightly minty aroma.

  • Bright green pods with strong fragrance
  • Popular in desserts, teas, and spice blends
  • Considered one of the most valuable spices in the world

Grades of Ceylon Cardamom

Ceylon cardamom is graded by pod size, color, and aroma. Larger, greener pods are considered more premium, while smaller or lighter pods are used mainly for grinding.

Extra Bold

Largest & Most Aromatic

Big, bright green pods with strong fragrance. Considered the highest grade, ideal for gourmet cooking, premium teas, and export.

Bold

Balanced Premium

Medium–large pods with a good aroma and uniform color. Perfect for everyday cooking, desserts, and spiced beverages.

Small / Light

Everyday Utility

Smaller, paler pods with mild aroma. Commonly used for grinding into spice blends, curry powders, and bulk food production.

Ceylon Cardamom vs Other Types

Feature Ceylon (Sri Lanka) Indian Guatemalan
Flavor Sweet, floral, delicate aroma Strong, spicy, earthy Pungent, bold, often used in commercial blends
Color Bright green pods Green to slightly yellowish pods Darker green to brownish pods
Size Medium to large, uniform Medium Variable, often larger but less aromatic
Typical Use Premium teas, desserts, fine cooking Traditional Indian sweets, curries, chai Commercial spice mixes, mass-market products

Tip: Bright green, aromatic pods with sweet floral notes are characteristic of Sri Lankan cardamom.

Storage & Freshness

Cardamom loses flavor if not stored properly. Here’s how to keep it fresh:

  • Store in an airtight container, away from sunlight
  • Keep in a cool, dry place
  • Whole pods stay fresher longer than ground cardamom

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ceylon cardamom better than other types?

Yes—Sri Lankan cardamom is considered more delicate and aromatic, making it highly prized in gourmet cooking.

Can I use Ceylon cardamom in baking?

Absolutely! It pairs wonderfully with cakes, cookies, and pastries.

How long does cardamom stay fresh?

Whole pods can retain flavor for up to 1 year if stored correctly, while ground cardamom lasts only a few months.
